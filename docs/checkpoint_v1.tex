\section*{Research Question}

Does replacing the standard guardian grid with an aesthetically-integrated
passthrough blend reduce cybersickness and presence disruption without degrading
safety outcomes? Concretely: participants who receive a DreamGuard-style
proximity cue will report lower SSQ scores and higher IPQ presence ratings than
those who receive the default Meta Quest guardian grid, with no significant
difference in logged near-miss or contact events.

\section*{Current Progress}

The DreamGuard prototype is running on a Meta Quest 3 in a Godot 4 VR dungeon
environment. At this stage, two core components are functional: a basic dungeon scene
and a basic windowed passthrough overlay. The dungeon scene provides a representative
VR environment for testing safety transitions (Figure~\ref{fig:dungeon}). The passthrough
component uses spatial shader-based compositing to cut a rectangular window into the
scene, revealing the physical environment through the headset's cameras. A menu system
allows toggling between passthrough configurations during a session (Figure~\ref{fig:menu}).
All other planned features---multiple blending styles, automated trigger logic, study
instrumentation---remain unimplemented and are the focus of future work.

\section*{Key Challenges}

Three non-trivial technical obstacles were encountered while reaching the current
prototype state: (1)~GPU frustum culling silently discarded the fullscreen passthrough
quads when the owning node sat at floor level, requiring a custom infinite bounding-box
override; (2)~the passthrough node unconditionally reset \texttt{transparent\_bg} to
\texttt{false} on deactivation, breaking the alpha compositing pipeline; and
(3)~Godot~4 Forward Mobile's multiview \texttt{SCREEN\_UV} maps to the combined stereo
render target rather than each eye independently, causing the passthrough window to
appear offset per eye and producing double vision. The third issue has not yet been
fully resolved and is under active investigation.

\section*{Plan for Next Checkpoint}

With a basic windowed passthrough and dungeon scene in place, the next checkpoint will
focus on: (1)~resolving the per-eye double-vision rendering issue and validating the fix
on device; (2)~implementing additional passthrough blending styles beyond the basic
window (e.g., vignette, animated fade) so that conditions can be meaningfully compared;
(3)~adding trigger logic and session instrumentation (logging of trigger events, blend
intensity over time, and active style condition); and (4)~finalizing the study design,
including resolving the within-session SSQ carryover concern and reframing the
no-warning control as a minimal-cue baseline to reduce IRB risk.
